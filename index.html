About

The Financial Transparency Group is a research center located at the Department of Computer Science (DIKU), University of Copenhagen. The group promotes applied, multidisciplinary research addressing computational and organisational challenges at the intersection of financial systems, distributed ledger technology, and regulatory compliance.

Our work spans the design and analysis of decentralised financial infrastructure, the governance of digital asset ecosystems, and the implications of blockchain technology for reporting, transparency, and market integrity. We collaborate with industry partners, regulatory bodies, and academic institutions, and accept applications from qualified PhD candidates and postdoctoral researchers on an ongoing basis.

Research Themes

The group's research addresses a broad set of problems in computational finance and digital asset infrastructure:

Decentralized Finance (DeFi)
Blockchain & Smart Contracts
Automated Market Makers
Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs)
Compliance & Regulatory Reporting
Digital Asset Governance
Post-Trade Processing
Financial Transparency & Market Integrity

Members

Omri Ross

Associate Professor — Department of Computer Science, University of Copenhagen

Omri Ross leads the Financial Transparency Group. His research focuses on decentralised finance (DeFi), automated market makers, and the application of blockchain technology to post-trade processing and financial contract execution. He has collaborated extensively with industry and regulatory stakeholders, and supervises PhD research in financial blockchain infrastructure.

Johannes Rude Jensen

Visiting Researcher — Department of Computer Science, University of Copenhagen

Johannes Rude Jensen's research centres on financial computation, distributed ledger technology, and DeFi infrastructure. His work examines post-trade settlement, automated market makers, NFT market integrity, and blockchain-based financial systems for emerging economies. He has published widely at leading information systems conferences and venues.

Michel Avital

Professor of Digitalization — Department of Digitalization, Copenhagen Business School

Michel Avital's research examines the intersections of digital innovation, organisational transformation, and emerging technology ecosystems. He has published extensively on blockchain-enabled business models, decentralised identity management, digital asset governance, and the social implications of information technology. He is an editorial board member of leading technology journals and a frequent organiser at major international IS conferences.

Nina-Birte Schirrmacher

Researcher — Financial Transparency Group

Nina-Birte Schirrmacher investigates the relationship between organisational practices and emerging technologies, with a focus on decentralised autonomous organisations (DAOs), token-driven governance, and accountability in decentralised systems. Her work also addresses the unintended consequences of digital innovation, including cybercrime and digital divides. She has presented her research at leading IS venues including ICIS and the Academy of Management Annual Meeting.
